— Executive Summary
Zone gating is working. River–Bay pacing lands close to target. Sea and Ocean costs (after the ×5 rod repricing in v0.8.1) are now steep enough to push F2P timelines well past 8 months.
Automation ROI is healthy. Net payback periods scale realistically (10c Net pays back in <2 min; 500M Fleet takes months), preventing early snowballing without making automation feel useless.
Diamond economy is thin. Sources are sparse and mostly passive (seagull, rare trophy catch). No daily reliable diamond faucet exists outside competition wins, creating long droughts for F2P players between Premium Bait uses.
Dynamic demand is unimplemented. CLAUDE.md describes a full demand cycle system (Crash / Low / Normal / High / Surge). Code sells all fish at flat base value with no price fluctuation. A core strategy layer is missing.
Competition duration mismatch. CLAUDE.md specifies 1-hour competitions; code runs them for 5 minutes. This is either a deliberate decision not reflected in documentation, or a critical bug.
Prestige resets automation. All automation — including per-zone nets, fishermen, boats, fleets, and expedition vessels — is wiped on prestige. This contradicts the Empire design pillar ("old zones never become obsolete") and is likely to feel punishing.
Audit scope: This report is derived from a static analysis of game.js, version.js, and zone JSON files as of v0.8.1 (Build 38). All code values cited are source-verified unless marked (est.).

Cost curve analysis

River → Lake is a 10× cost step; Lake → Bay is another 10×. These feel consistent. The Bay → Sea jump is ~97× (1.5M → 145M), which is the sharpest single step in the game and will be the most common "wall" players hit. This is likely intentional as a monetisation pressure point, but it needs to feel earned through content, not arbitrary.

Bay rod (900,000c) vs. Sea Rod (125,000,000c): The gap between adjacent rods is 139×. No other rod-to-rod jump exceeds 15×. This is the largest outlier in the cost ladder and may cause players to feel stuck even after fully automating Bay.

Fleet ROI concern: Small and Large Fleets have payback periods of 25–63 minutes at Sea catch values. If Sea income is as estimated (~1M c/min per Motor Boat), a player with even moderate Bay automation stacked could reach Sea and buy a Small Fleet within hours of unlocking the zone. This risks compressing the Sea → Ocean timeline significantly. Recommend monitoring first with live data before nerfing further.

Automation stacking: the +22% cost curve

Each additional unit of the same type costs 22% more than the previous. This creates a natural soft cap — buying 10 Fishing Nets costs a total of ~1,750c, 20 nets costs ~18,000c. The curve steepens but never hard-caps, which is correct for an empire builder. No concern here.

F2P diamond drought problem: A casual F2P player (no Season Pass, no IAP) earns roughly 3–12◆ per day from Lost Treasure events and occasional trophy catches. Premium Bait costs 5◆. This means F2P players can activate Premium Bait roughly once per day — which is healthy. However, cosmetic bobbers cost 15–250◆, making them achievable over weeks to months. The C'thulu Bobber (250◆) would take a F2P player ~1–3 months to save for. This may frustrate players who want to show cosmetics but are far from the Abyss prestige engine.
Season Pass free-tier diamonds: CLAUDE.md specifies ~28◆/month on the free tier. This supplements F2P earnings significantly and should be considered a baseline assumption for F2P experience.

Ghost Busters pearl upgrade interaction

Each Ghost Busters level reduces expedition time by 3,600,000ms / 24 = 150,000ms = 2.5 real minutes. At max level 20, the expedition shortens by 50 real minutes — reducing from 1 hour to 10 real minutes. Pearl costs for Ghost Busters: 10 → 20 → 40 → ... → 5,242,880 (max total ~10.5M pearls for all 20 levels).

Ghost Ship balance verdict: The automation-unit reward replaces coin-only or diamond rewards and ensures every Ghost Ship interaction meaningfully advances the player's empire. Coin reward of 24–48h of income is generous but not game-breaking since it scales with current automation state.
Ancient unit prestige reset: Ancient Fishermen and Ancient Fishing Boats earned from Ghost Ship are wiped on prestige alongside all other automation. Ghost Ship units are harder to earn (RNG-gated behind a 7h spawn cycle) than purchasable automation — losing them on prestige may feel especially painful. Consider exempting ghost-only units from prestige reset, or making the prestige screen explicitly show what will be lost.

What resets on prestige (all of it):
  • All coins earned
  • All automation owned (Nets, Fishermen, Boats, Fleets, Ancient units)
  • All Expedition Vessels
  • Ghost Ship state
  • Zone unlocks (re-gated by zone cost multiplier)

What persists: Pearls, Pearl Upgrades, Fishdex completion, Achievements, Cosmetics, Competition titles/Hall of Fame.

This is a full wipe prestige model. Combined with the "Empire" design pillar (zones should never become obsolete), this creates a contradiction: the game tells players to build a multi-zone empire, then resets it entirely on prestige. This tension should be documented as a deliberate decision with rationale (if intended), or re-evaluated.

Ocean unlock cost analysis (critical path)

Ocean Rod costs 1,250,000,000c (1.25B) after the ×5 repricing. Research Vessel costs 200,000,000c (200M). Total: 1.45B coins.

At Sea zone automation income (rough estimate with a Fishing Boat: ~4M catches × 5,000c avg = ~20B c/hr for a deep automation stack — but that's endgame Sea automation which itself costs hundreds of millions to build).

A reasonable mid-Sea player income might be ~2–5M c/hr (active + automation combined). At 5M c/hr × 2h/day = 10M c/day. To earn 1.45B c at 10M c/day takes 145 days ≈ 5 months. That is on top of the ~5–8 months to reach Sea. Total F2P Ocean timeline: 10–13 months, which is above the 8-month target but within range for dedicated players.

However, for casual players (1h/day, 30% active), the timeline could extend to 18–24 months. This is likely too long and will cause F2P churn before Ocean is ever seen.

The ×5 rod repricing from v0.8.1 pushed Ocean Rod from 250M to 1.25B. This may have overcorrected. Sea and Ocean rod costs already had a ×3 increase from the June 25 overhaul. Stacking ×5 on top makes Ocean Rod alone more expensive than what a player has accumulated through all previous zones combined (Cumulative to Bay: ~1.67M). Recommend analyzing whether the ×5 should apply only to Sea Rod, or whether Ocean+ should be a separate, smaller multiplier.

Dynamic demand is the most impactful missing feature. CLAUDE.md describes it as a "core strategy mechanic" and it underlies the spoilage tension loop (the reason to upgrade cooling storage). Without demand fluctuation, players have no reason to time sells — they sell immediately at flat prices. Storage upgrades lose their strategic justification beyond raw capacity. This system should be on the immediate implementation backlog.

What is working well

  • Onboarding (first 5 minutes): First Net at 10c with a 60s catch rate gets automation started within 1 minute. Excellent tutorial hook.
  • Automation stacking design: Per-unit +22% escalating cost creates a natural feel of building an empire without hard caps.
  • Ghost Ship reward redesign (v0.8.1): Automation units as rewards directly advance the empire fantasy and feel meaningful every time.
  • Rod click-reduction system: Reducing taps-per-catch (20→4) creates immediately visible rod progression — a superior alternative to hidden percentage bonuses.
  • Pearl upgrade costs for Ghost Busters: Doubling cost curve with max level ensures it's a long-term endgame investment, not a trivial shortcut.

What needs attention

  • No dynamic demand: The most design-significant missing system. Spoilage, storage upgrades, and market timing all hinge on it.
  • Ocean Rod overcorrected: The v0.8.1 ×5 repricing may have pushed Ocean Rod too far (1.25B). Consider validating with live data before it reaches players who are currently in Sea zone.
  • Prestige full reset: Conflicts with stated Empire design philosophy. Even if full reset is intentional, it should be presented as a deliberate design choice with clear player communication, not an implicit consequence.
  • Competition is 5 min, not 1 hour: If this is intentional, CLAUDE.md must be updated. If it's a bug, it breaks the competition reward ecosystem (only top 50 can win in 5 minutes).
09 Recommendations
R-01
Critical Implement dynamic demand system
The fish market demand cycle (Crash/Low/Normal/High/Surge) is the core strategy loop described in CLAUDE.md. Without it, spoilage has no strategic tension, storage upgrades lose their key selling point, and the market screen is purely informational. Priority: highest before next zone content.
R-02
Critical Decide and document competition duration
Code: 5 minutes. Spec: 1 hour. This is a binary decision. At 5 minutes, competitions are a quick daily challenge (casual-friendly). At 1 hour, they are a commitment session (hardcore-friendly). Either is valid, but they require very different reward structures and notification timing. Decide, then update the documentation and competition notification text.
R-03
High Re-evaluate Ocean Rod ×5 pricing
Ocean Rod at 1.25B coins may push F2P Ocean timeline past 24 months. Suggest reverting to ×2 or ×3 on Ocean Rod specifically (while keeping Sea Rod at ×5), giving a Sea unlock cost of ~145M and Ocean of ~700M–900M. This preserves the intended ~8-month Ocean target for dedicated F2P players.
R-04
High Clarify prestige reset scope in player-facing UI
The prestige confirmation screen should explicitly list what resets (coins, all automation, zones) and what persists (pearls, fishdex, cosmetics, achievements). Players who have built a multi-zone empire for months will be devastated by an unexpected full reset. Transparency prevents churn. Consider also whether some automation should survive (e.g., Pond-level nets persist as "sentimental" — it aligns with the Empire pillar).
R-05
High Protect Ghost Ship–earned automation from prestige reset
Ancient Fisherman and Ancient Fishing Boat units are earned through rare, time-gated Ghost Ship expeditions (60% chance every 7 real hours). Wiping them on prestige undervalues the player's investment and breaks the "only way to get these" exclusivity of the ghost-only units. These units could persist across prestiges as a permanent fleet addition — they're not purchasable anyway, so they don't break economy balance.
R-06
Medium Add a daily reliable diamond source for F2P players
Currently F2P diamond income is sparse (Lost Treasure events, sporadic trophy catches). Consider a small daily login reward (1–2◆/day) or a daily quest that rewards 2–3◆. This gives F2P players a predictable cadence to budget toward Premium Bait (5◆) or cosmetics, reducing the feeling of diamond drought without disrupting the IAP revenue model.
R-07
Medium Remove or repurpose the automation chest probability code
Automation chest probability is ~1.16e-9 per catch — at 600 catches/second (max Deep Sea Fleet), this still triggers less than once per week of continuous running. With a 7-hour cooldown on top, this code path likely never fires in practice. Either raise the probability to something meaningful (>0.001%) or remove the dead code path to reduce confusion.
R-08
Low Sync CLAUDE.md with all v0.8.x code changes
Premium Bait cost (2◆ in old notes vs. 5◆ in code), auto-sell interval (undocumented), competition duration (1h doc vs. 5min code), and the Phase 2 checklist (dynamic demand marked complete but not implemented) should all be corrected. Accurate documentation prevents future audit confusion and saves onboarding time for new contributors.
