01 Overview & Design Pillars

Patient Angler is a 2D idle/incremental fishing empire game for Android (Google Play). The player begins alone at the Pond with a Basic Rod and builds a layered fishing empire across six water bodies. Active fishing and idle automation (Nets → Fishermen → Boats → Fleets) run permanently side by side in every unlocked zone.

Engine: HTML5/JS (no framework) wrapped in Capacitor 7 for Android. · Developer: Recapsa OÜ / Henly Games.

Design Pillars
  1. Variable reward loop — players never know what they'll catch; rarity is the core addiction mechanic.
  2. Visible progress — each rod tier directly reduces taps-to-catch; upgrades feel immediate, not hidden in percentages.
  3. Active + idle balance — active fishing gives rarer catches and Trophy chances; automation keeps every owned zone earning permanently.
  4. Time-of-day mechanics — some species only appear during specific in-game hours, creating daily check-in motivation.
  5. Competitions — scheduled daily tournaments create retention and social hooks.
  6. Collection drive — the 180-entry Fishdex drives completion motivation.
  7. Empire growth, not zone replacement — all unlocked zones produce simultaneously; players stack income, never migrate.
  8. Offline rewards — every owned zone earns while the player is away, capped at storage capacity.
02 Core Gameplay Loop

Active loop: Tap water → cast animation + sound → bobber floats → bobber dips → tap bobber N times within window → catch revealed → sell / store → upgrade → repeat.

N (taps required) is set by the equipped rod: 20 taps for the Basic Rod, decreasing to 4 taps for the Abyss Rod. Missing the tap window lets the fish escape. Sound effects play on cast, bobber dip, and successful catch.

Fish Fight Events Active

During active fishing there is a 0.5% chance per eligible catch (non-trash, non-plant, non-trophy) of triggering a Fish Fight. A competing fish tries to steal the catch — the player must tap a depleting bar (25 taps, 10-second window). Win = catch at ×5 base value. Lose = catch forfeited. A 3-minute cooldown (180 000 ms) prevents back-to-back triggers.

Sea & Ocean Bobber View v0.8

In the Sea and Ocean zones only, casting overlays a dedicated background image (boat-eye ocean view) behind the bobber animation, positioned at the bobber area. The image closes automatically when fishing ends or is reset. All other mechanics are identical to other zones.

Auto-Sell

When active (permanent via Diamond purchase or temporary via a special event), every caught fish sells immediately at current market price. Trophy fish are always exempt — they always go to storage regardless of Auto-Sell state.

  • Special catch fanfare animation

Storage is stackable — ownedStorage is an array; all purchased tiers add their capacity together. Bay Rod tier upgrade gives +12% storage per tier. Pearl Expanded Holds gives +50% per Pearl upgrade level. Both stack multiplicatively with base capacity.

Storage cap
When full, automation pauses and offline progress stops being recorded. New catches cannot be added until space is freed. There is no fish spoilage or expiry — fish stored indefinitely.

Fish are stored in a pile system (G.fishPile keyed by fishId|size) — stacked by species+size, not individual items. Trophy fish go to a separate G.trophyPile array with full individual metadata.

Events fire every 15–30 minutes while the app is in the foreground. An icon animates in from the screen edge. The player taps to open a claim popup. Unclaimed events expire after ~90 seconds, then the next event is scheduled. Multiple buffs can be active simultaneously.

16 Diamonds & Premium Bait

Diamonds (◆) are the premium currency. Players start with 5 Diamonds. Earned via Trophy catches (+1 each), Lost Treasure events (+3 each), quests, and IAP. Spent on Premium Bait (5 ◆) and bobber cosmetics.

Premium Bait — 5 ◆ / activation, 30 min

Doubles the weight of all non-trash, non-plant, non-common entries in the loot table. Common probability drops proportionally. Does not guarantee rare catches — improves expected value.

18 Daily Competition & Hall of Fame
Competition Format

Players compete to catch the largest single fish (by Trophy value). Active fishing only — automation disabled during competition. Player must own the required gear for the competition zone. 9 AI bots populate the leaderboard.

Production duration: 60 minutes. Prototype duration (current build): 5 minutes (COMP_DURATION_MS = 300 000 ms).

Series System

Every 10 competitions form a named Series. Points are awarded per placement: 10/7/5/3/2/1 (rank 1–6; rank 7–10 get 1 point; beyond 10 get 0). After 10 competitions, a Grand Series winners screen is shown.

19 Fish Encyclopedia (Fishdex)

Every catchable item — fish, plant, trash — has a Fishdex entry. Total: 180 entries (6 zones × 30 items) plus the time-window Special fish in a separate section. Entries are preserved across prestige resets.

The Fishdex screen has zone tabs. Caught entries show the species image and a rarity dot; uncaught entries show a silhouette. A progress counter (X / 30) appears per zone. Plants and trash are recorded automatically on catch even though they are discarded.

Each entry tracks: firstCaughtDate, totalCatches, largestWeight (physical grams), bestSize, trophyCount. Personal record weight is shown on the entry card and updated whenever a heavier specimen is caught.

Mastery data
Per-fish mastery is stored in G.masteryData and G.manualFishdex. These accumulate across all prestige resets and are never cleared.
20 Prestige & Black Pearls
Prestige Reset

Resets: Coins · All automation · Storage tier · Transport vehicles · Zone access (back to Pond only) · Rod tier levels · Bobber tier levels · Active buffs.

Preserved: Fishdex entries · Mastery data · Diamonds · Black Pearls · Pearl upgrade levels · Prestige count · Cosmetics · Competition history · Quest/achievement progress.

Pearl reward formula: floor(sqrt(coins / 2000)). Examples: 15,000c → 2 Pearls · 50,000c → 5 Pearls · 200,000c → 10 Pearls · 1,000,000c → 22 Pearls. Minimum 1 Pearl.
